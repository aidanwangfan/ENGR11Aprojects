Project 2

Introduction

As I moved forward in learning about 3D design and printing, I learned how to remix and modify existing 3D models using CAD (Computer-Aided Design) tools like Tinkercad and Autodesk Fusion. For this project, I ran two different 3D prints, one using Tinkercad and the other using Autodesk Fusion, to compare how different they were from each other. By using these 2 different tools, I gained a better understanding of editing and remixing 3D files, which helped me get better at solving design problems and creating new ideas.

Idea and sketch - Tinkercad

Thinking back to my childhood, I decided to make a Pokemon-inspired design. I’ve always loved Pokemon, and one of my favourite characters has always been Squirtle. With that in mind, I wanted to create something that captured nostalgia in a fun and creative way. To begin, I browsed several websites, including Thangs.com, Thingiverse.com, and Printables.com for inspiration and found a Pokeball model that I could use as a base. I also found a Squirtle figure that fit perfectly with my idea.

Tinkercad Remix (Preparation before printing)

  1. 1. Download the Squirtle and Pokemon as STL files and import them into Tinkercad.
  2. 2. Add a cylinder base into the Pokeball to make it easier for the Squirtle to stand.
  3. 3. Drag the Squirtle into the Pokeball and adjust to make it fit perfectly.
  4. 4. Group all the items together using the “Group” feature on Tinkercad.
  5. 5. Download the 3D model file and edit it in Prusa Slicer.
  6. 6. Adjust the settings, including layer height, infill percentage, and more.
  7. 7. Based on past experiences, I added organic supports for easier removal.
  8. 8. Slice the file and check that all the supports are perfectly aligned.
  9. 9. Export the G-code to a USB drive formatted for the printer and name the file appropriately.
  10. 10. Insert the USB into the printer and navigate to the file in the printer menu.
  11. 11. Start the print and monitor the first few layers to ensure proper adhesion.

Reflection for Tinkercad Print

As it was my first time using Tinkercad, it took a bit of time to manoeuvre through. However, with a bit of practice, I started getting the hang of it and the process went smoothly. The final print turned out just how I envisioned it, with smooth surfaces and a solid structure. That said, there’s definitely room for improvement. Next time, I could definitely try multi-color printing to bring Squirtle and the Pokeball to life. Furthermore, I would also spend more time in Tinkercad refining the shape to ensure the Pokeball prints as a perfect sphere rather than coming out slightly flat. Overall, this project was a super fun mix of creativity and problem-solving, and I am looking forward to making even better prints in the future.

CAM Settings for First Print:

  • Used Filament (g): 105.74
  • Filament: Prusament PLA
  • Printing Time: 6 Hours and 39 Minutes
  • Layer Height: 0.2mm
  • Infill Percentage: 15%

Idea and Sketch - Fusion

Thinking back to my love for tennis, I wanted to create a design that reflected both my passion for the sport and something a bit personal. Since I’ve been playing tennis since I was five and now play on the varsity men’s tennis team at Brandeis University, I thought it would be fun to incorporate that into my 3D project. I decided to use Mewtwo as the main character because it has such a strong, dynamic vibe, which seemed like a perfect fit for holding a tennis racket. My goal was to make a design that’s eye-catching and playful, blending the energy of tennis with my love for Pokémon.

Reflection

Both prints taught me a lot about 3D printing. The name tag was a great way to get the hang of slicing and printing, while the torture test really showed me how tricky it can be to print more complex designs. Looking ahead, I plan to tweak my settings, especially for supports and cooling, to improve the overall print quality.

With each project, I'm feeling more confident using the Prusa MINI+ and getting better at the whole process.

I’m really excited to keep experimenting with new designs and learn even more about 3D printing along the way!
