Tinkercad and Fusion

A project exploring the process of remixing public 3D files into unique and functional 3D prints. This journey includes selecting a public 3D model and importing it into CAD tools like Tinkercad or Autodesk Fusion (formerly Fusion360), where new features are added or different models are combined to create innovative designs. Reflections on the project emphasize mastering the workflow of editing and remixing 3D files, solving design challenges, and enhancing skills in CAD and 3D printing.

Form and Fit for Electronics

A project exploring the process of designing and iterating on a custom chassis enclosure for an electronic prototype. This journey involves taking precise measurements of a robot kit, using CAD tools like Autodesk Fusion 360 to model an enclosure, and fabricating it through both 3D printing and laser cutting. Reflections on the project emphasize mastering the design process, problem-solving for fit and function, and enhancing skills in CAD modeling, and electronics prototyping.

Open-source assistive technology

A project exploring the process of 3D printing and assembling an open-source prosthetic hand. This experience involves using the e-NABLE hub and NIH 3D Print Exchange to source models, preparing files with Fusion 360 and Prusa Slicer, and fabricating a 150% scale prosthesis with a Prusa Mini+ 3D printer. Reflections on the project focus on understanding assistive technology, improving documentation for assembly, and learning the role of rapid prototyping in design and accessibility.
