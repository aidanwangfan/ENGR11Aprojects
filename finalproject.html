Problem Definition

Learning to play the guitar can be frustrating for beginners due to the physical demands of finger placement and chord memorization. This challenge is even greater for individuals with hand pain, limited mobility, or other physical difficulties that make playing techniques harder to manage. Many aspiring players, including beginners and players with hand-related problems, often feel discouraged and give up early in the process.

Guitar Buddy aims to solve this problem by providing an assistive device that helps players learn chords faster, and more effortlessly. By simplifying the physical strain of learning guitar, it makes playing more accessible, and enjoyable for everyone–including those with hand problems.

Prototyping & Testing

Using our storyboard, my group and I pitched our product to our classmates and professors. By doing so, we were able to collect feedback and questions that could possibly improve our product. We noticed a couple patterns in the feedback we received, such as, adaptability for users, and how the product could damage the guitar. Using all the amazing feedback and ideas, we were able to create our first Feedback Capture Grid!

Feedback Capture Grid #1

Feedback Capture Grid #1

Prototype Iteration 1

For our initial prototype, we developed a device that enables users to play three major chords by pressing a single button for each chord. We began by 3D printing an open source model and testing it on our group's guitar to identify any necessary adjustments for future iterations. Once we confirmed that the device fit securely on the guitar neck, we conducted an interview with another group to gather user feedback. During the session, we encouraged them to interact with the prototype, ask questions, and share their thoughts. Prototype Iteration 2

After gathering feedback on our initial prototype, we used the insights to inform the development of our second iteration. This time, we concentrated on enhancing the product’s adjustability. In particular, we explored ways to allow the device to slide along the guitar neck, enabling users to play a wider range of chords and ensuring compatibility with various guitar styles. We then shared this updated version with another project group, inviting them to test it out and share their thoughts.
